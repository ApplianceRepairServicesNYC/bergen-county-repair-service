Common Appliance Emergencies We Handle

Refrigerator failures are among the most urgent appliance emergencies. When your refrigerator or freezer stops cooling, food spoilage begins within hours, potentially resulting in hundreds of dollars in lost groceries. Our technicians respond quickly to refrigeration emergencies, diagnosing cooling problems and implementing repairs to save your food and prevent further damage. Common refrigerator emergencies include complete cooling failure, freezer defrost issues, compressor malfunctions, and refrigerant leaks.

Washer and water-related emergencies require immediate attention to prevent water damage to your home. Whether your washer is leaking, won't drain, or has overflowed, we can dispatch a technician to stop the problem and make repairs. Quick response to water-related issues can save you from costly floor, wall, and structural damage. We've seen situations where delayed response led to thousands of dollars in water damage, which is why we prioritize these calls.

Gas appliance emergencies are particularly serious due to safety concerns. If you smell gas near your range, oven, or dryer, turn off the appliance immediately, ventilate the area, and contact us for emergency service. Our technicians are trained to handle gas appliance repairs safely and ensure all connections meet current codes before completing any repair. Never ignore a gas smell, as this could indicate a dangerous leak requiring immediate professional attention.

Dryer emergencies often involve overheating, which can create fire hazards. If your dryer is producing excessive heat, burning smells, or unusual sounds, stop using it immediately and call for emergency service. Lint buildup, failed thermostats, and blocked venting can all create dangerous conditions that our technicians can identify and resolve quickly.

What to Do in an Appliance Emergency

If you're experiencing an appliance emergency, take these immediate steps to protect yourself and your home. For refrigerator failures, avoid opening the doors unnecessarily ‚Äì a closed refrigerator can maintain safe temperatures for several hours, and a closed freezer can stay cold for up to 48 hours if full. Transfer the most perishable items to a cooler with ice if available, and note the time the problem started.

For water leaks, turn off the water supply to the appliance if possible and use towels or a wet vacuum to contain the water. If the leak is significant, also turn off the main water supply to your home. Move any items that could be damaged by water away from the affected area, and take photos for insurance purposes if necessary.

For gas-related emergencies, safety comes first. If you smell gas, don't operate any electrical switches or create any sparks. Open windows and doors to ventilate, leave the area, and call us from outside the home. We'll dispatch a technician immediately to address the situation safely. If the gas smell is strong, you may also want to contact your gas company's emergency line.

For electrical emergencies involving appliances that are sparking, smoking, or producing burning smells, unplug the appliance immediately if safe to do so, or turn off the circuit breaker. Do not attempt to use the appliance again until it has been professionally inspected and repaired.

Types of Emergency Repairs We Perform

Our emergency repair capabilities cover the full spectrum of household appliances. For refrigerators and freezers, we can address compressor failures, refrigerant leaks, thermostat malfunctions, defrost system problems, and electrical issues. Our technicians carry diagnostic equipment to quickly identify the problem and common replacement parts to complete many repairs on the spot.

For washers, our emergency services include repairing drain pump failures, addressing water inlet valve problems, fixing lid switch and door lock issues, and resolving motor and transmission problems. We also handle emergency repairs for control board failures that can leave your washer inoperable at the worst possible times.

Dryer emergencies we handle include heating element replacements, thermostat repairs, motor issues, and drum problems. We also address ventilation issues that can create fire hazards and help customers understand proper dryer maintenance to prevent future emergencies.

For cooking appliances, our emergency services cover gas valve repairs, igniter replacements, heating element failures, temperature control issues, and electrical problems. We treat all gas appliance emergencies with the utmost care and attention to safety protocols.
